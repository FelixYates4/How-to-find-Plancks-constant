Measuring Planck's Constant Using LEDs

Method 1 Method 2 Summary

Method 1: Using Energy and Frequency

The energy of a photon can be calculated using the formula:

E = h * f

Experimental Setup:

  1. Calculate the energy (E) for each LED using the threshold voltage (V) and the elementary charge (e):
    2. E = e * V
  2. Calculate the frequency (f) from the wavelength (λ):
    3. f = c / λ

Graph:

Plot energy (E) on the Y-axis against frequency (f) on the X-axis. The slope of the trendline will equal Planck's constant (h).

Method 2: Using Threshold Voltage and Frequency

This method uses the relationship between threshold voltage and photon energy:

e * V = h * f

Experimental Setup:

  1. Measure the threshold voltage (V) for each LED.
  2. Calculate the frequency (f) from the wavelength.

Graph:

Plot threshold voltage (V) on the Y-axis against frequency (f) on the X-axis. The slope of the trendline will provide h / e. To find Planck's constant:

h = m * e
where m is the slope of the line.

Summary

Both methods yield the value of Planck's constant (h) but rely on different relationships:

  • Energy Method: Focuses on the energy of emitted photons.
  • Threshold Voltage Method: Uses the voltage needed to emit light from the LED.

Using either method should yield consistent values for Planck's constant, provided that measurements and calculations are accurate.
